# Noom Analysis - What We Liked

## Noom Goal Setting Approach

### Core Principles

#### SMART Goal Framework
- Utilizes SMART goals framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Guides users in setting structured, attainable goals

#### Emphasis on Psychology
- Psychology-based approach to understand the "why" behind eating habits
- Builds healthier routines for lasting results
- Goes beyond just tracking food and exercise

#### Individualized Plans
- Creates personalized weight loss and fitness plans
- Based on user's:
  - Health status
  - Demographics
  - Goals
  - Lifestyle

#### Breaking Down Goals
- Encourages breaking larger goals into smaller milestones
- Provides sense of accomplishment
- Builds momentum throughout journey

#### Focus on Behavior Change
- Develops sustainable habits related to:
  - Nutrition
  - Exercise
  - Sleep
  - Stress management
  - Self-care

#### Tracking and Feedback
- Users track food intake and exercise
- Receive feedback from app and coaches
- Helps make better choices to reach target weight

## Types of Goals in Noom

### Weight Goal
- Establishes clear target weight
- Initially calculated based on NIH and National Diabetes Prevention Plan recommendations
- Allows for adjustments

### Step Goal
- Encourages increasing physical activity
- Initially sets goal of 2,000 steps
- Adjusts based on user activity

### Nutrition Goals
- Color-coded food system (green, yellow, orange)
- Guides users toward nutrient-dense foods
- Encourages healthier food choices

### Lifestyle Goals
- Extends beyond weight loss to overall wellness
- Goals for:
  - Improved sleep
  - Stress management
  - Other healthy habits

## Key Features We Like

### The Promise
- Uses technology to change how you think, not just how you eat
- Psychology-based approach to change eating habits
- More than 50 million downloads since 2013

### Comprehensive Wellness Plan
- Food tracking
- Exercise monitoring
- Mental health aspects
- Long-term behavior change focus

### No Forbidden Foods
- No foods are off-limits
- Color-coding system:
  - **Green**: Most nutrients, bulk of diet (vegetables, fruits, whole grains)
  - **Yellow**: Moderate nutrients and calories (lean proteins, legumes)
  - **Orange**: Highest calories, eat less often (oils, nuts, nut butters)

### Technology Integration
- Built-in pedometer
- Exercise recommendations
- Daily articles about nutrition and weight loss
- Interactive challenges

### Support System
- Personal coach through messaging
- Virtual support groups
- Daily guidance and encouragement
- Behavior-change specialists

### Flexibility
- Accommodates various dietary preferences
- Vegan to gluten-free options
- Personalized recipes and meal suggestions
