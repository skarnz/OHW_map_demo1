# Behavior Change and Health Milestones

## Overview
Each participant's milestone will be individualized based on their health needs and preferences. The OHW program is tailored to be culturally competent and adapted to a person's life and life events as they move towards optimizing their health.

## Major Milestones

### Initial Setup and Goal Setting
- **Inputting Personal Information**: Age, height, current weight, and target weight
- **Setting Weight Loss Goals**: Defining realistic weekly or monthly weight loss targets (e.g., 1-2 pounds per week)
- **Choosing a Diet Plan or Approach**: Selecting dietary preferences or plans aligned with individual needs
- **Linking to Wearable Devices**: Connecting with fitness trackers (Fitbit, Apple Watch) for automated activity and sleep tracking

### Tracking and Progress
- **Daily Calorie Tracking**: Logging food intake and monitoring daily calorie goals
- **Activity Tracking**: Recording workouts, steps, and other physical activities
- **Weight Tracking**: Regularly recording weight to monitor progress against goals
- **Body Measurements**: Tracking changes in body measurements (waist, hips, etc.) to assess fat loss
- **Achievement Badges and Rewards**: Earning badges or virtual rewards for hitting milestones

### Achieving Milestones and Celebrating
- **Reaching Smaller Weight Loss Goals**: Celebrating milestones like losing 1, 2, or 5 pounds
- **Reaching Larger Weight Loss Goals**: Celebrating larger milestones like losing 10, 20, or 50 pounds
- **Reaching Goal Weight**: Celebrating the achievement of the final target weight
- **Sustaining Weight Loss**: Tracking progress and maintaining a healthy weight over time

### Other Important Milestones
- **Consistently Logging Food and Activity**: Developing a habit of tracking all food and activity for a set period (e.g., 30 days)
- **Completing Exercise Challenges**: Achieving specific exercise goals (e.g., running a 5k, completing a workout plan)
- **Improving Fitness Metrics**: Noticing improvements in fitness metrics (e.g., increased step count, improved heart rate)
- **Building Sustainable Habits**: Developing healthy habits that can be maintained long-term

## Micro Milestones

### Food & Nutrition
- Successfully replacing sugary drinks with water
- Consistently incorporating more fruits and vegetables into daily meals
- Mastering portion control and being mindful of serving sizes
- Reducing reliance on processed and packaged foods
- Successfully planning and preparing healthy meals in advance
- Limiting sugary snacks and choosing healthier alternatives
- Achieving a daily water intake goal (e.g., 8 cups)
- Drinking water before meals to help manage appetite
- Incorporating water-rich foods like cucumber, watermelon, and strawberries

### Weight & Body Composition
- Reaching specific weight loss targets (e.g., 5% or 10% of body weight)
- Fitting into a smaller clothing size as a visible marker of progress
- Achieving a healthier body fat percentage
- Losing inches from waistline indicating reduction in abdominal/visceral fat
- Increasing muscle mass to decrease fat percentages

### Sleep
- Experiencing improved sleep quality and duration
- Reduced symptoms of sleep apnea, if applicable

### Mental Health
- Increased energy levels and motivation
- Improved mood and reduction in stress, anxiety, or depression
- Improved body image and self-esteem
- Developing healthy coping mechanisms for stress management that don't involve food

### Meeting Health Metrics & Reducing Health Risks (Medical Milestones)
- Achieving healthy blood pressure readings
- Improving cholesterol levels (LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
- Better blood sugar control, especially for individuals with diabetes
- Reducing or eliminating the need for certain medications
- Completing scheduled laboratory tests as recommended by healthcare provider
- Attending all scheduled healthcare provider appointments

### Fitness
- Increased endurance during physical activity
- Improved strength and muscle tone
- Completing fitness goals (e.g., running a race, finishing a challenging workout)
- Improved mobility and range of motion

### Changing Health Habits
- Completing Smart Micro Goals
- Establishing a consistent exercise routine
- Adopting healthy eating habits and practicing mindful eating
- Effectively managing stress and incorporating stress-reducing activities
- Prioritizing adequate sleep for overall health and well-being
- Reducing sedentary behavior and incorporating more movement into daily routine
- Using hydration tracking apps or other methods to monitor water intake
- Proactively scheduling and attending medical appointments and lab tests
